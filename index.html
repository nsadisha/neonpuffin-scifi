Year 2021 in sci-fi

Herein lie the bulk of the most anticipated sci-fi films of 2021 and beyond lined up for your perusal in order of their release. With the COVID-19 outbreak wreaking havoc on the film release dates, we take a look at 35 titles we hope to see on the big screen this year or in the near future.

  • The Three-Body Problem

    David Benioff and D.B. Weiss’s first project on Netflix will be a TV adaptation of Liu Cixin’s acclaimed sci-fi trilogy The Three-Body Problem.

    The television series will portray an alternative recent past and distant future of our planet where the Earth is preparing for an arrival of a large invasion fleet of a hostile and superior ancient alien civilisation from Alpha Centauri.

    Continue Reading »

  • Alien (Marvel Comics)

    The terrifying world of the ultimate nightmare - Xenomorph, comes at last to Marvel Comics in an all-new limited comic book series by a duo Phillip Kennedy Johnson and Salvador Larroca.

    The comic will take us to never-before-seen dark corners of the legendary Alien universe, as assured by Larroca's sinister pledge:
    "I promise you: our nightmares will be yours".

    Continue Reading »

  • Starfield

    As we are edging ever closer to the release date of Bethesda’s first new original franchise in over 25 years, Starfield, we recapitulate all that we know about the upcoming single-player, science fiction role playing game and tell you why we think it will bear all the hallmarks of game studio’s other successful product lines.

    We can hardly wait to lay our hands on it.

    Continue Reading »


  • Tenet

    Equally thrilling and confusing, Tenet delivers on its promise of deep intrigue and nail-biting temporal action, but you look away for so much as a second and you have lost the film's plot.

    Continue Reading »

  • Attack on Titan

    The final season of the Attack on Titan anime series charts Marley’s attempt to conquer Paradis Island, via the arcs ‘Marley’ and ‘War for Paradis’ (chapters 91–on) of the manga series.

    Continue Reading »

  • Baron Vladimir Harkonnen

    A gravity-defying monster of a man, gliding through the air like an embodiment of vice, Baron Vladimir Harkonnen is a gluttonous force of evil. Now available as a 12-inch figurine.

    Continue Reading »


  • DisCon III in Washington, D.C.

    25-29 August 2021

    The 79th World Science Fiction Convention also known as DisCon III, host of the 2021 Hugo Awards, will be held from Wednesday 25th to Sunday 29th August 2021 at the Marriott Wardman Park Hotel in Washington, D.C., USA.

    Arkady Martine

    Ever-expanding galactic empire with innate political instability at its heart, self-aware city controlled by A.I., human clones an integral part of society – Arkady Martine’s debut novel bears an uncanny resemblance to Ann Leckie’s early works.
