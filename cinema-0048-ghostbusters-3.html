Ghostbusters reboot cast confirmed

If there's something strange, in your neighbourhood, who you gonna call? If there's something weird, and it don't look good, who you gonna call?

For a complete reboot of the Ghostbusters franchise, film director Paul Feig decided to call a cast of four female actresses: Kristen Wiig and Melissa McCarthy of Bridesmaids fame, who will be joined by Saturday Night Live’s regular members Kate McKinnon and Leslie Jones.

After the phenomenal success of the romantic comedy Bridesmaids with six women in the main cast, the film’s director Paul Feig decided to do the all-female Ghostbusters restart, as revealed in Entertainment Weekly in 2014:

“Ghostbusters is such a great thing and everybody knows it. It's a shame to just let this thing sit there. I was like, what if it was an all female cast? If they were all women? Suddenly, my mind kind of exploded: that would be really fun!”

Feig added: “I don’t want to do a shot by shot update of a movie that existed. It’s the difficult thing about remaking a great movie. So that’s why we’re not remaking a great movie. We’re doing our take on it.”

Saturday Night Live is a highly popular US television comedy and variety show, so it is hoped that its regulars, comediennes Kate McKinnon and Leslie Jones will help to inject the required silliness into the planned reboot.

In the 1984 original Ghostbusters, the ghost-hunting quartet was portrayed by Bill Murray, Dan Aykroyd, Harold Ramis and Ernie Hudson, with Aykroyd and Ramis also co-writing the film.

Directed by Ivan Reitman, the film was an instant critical and commercial success, grossing more than $295 million worldwide and spawning a huge franchise comprising television series, video games, comic books, action figures and even a theme park.

A sequel, Ghostbusters II was released five years later in 1989 to a mixed reception, but still managed to make more than $215 million at the global box office on a relatively modest $37 million budget, making it a commercial success.

Multiple plans for a third film in the series have been proposed ever since, with the script written, and then rewritten, several times, but appeared to have been hampered by Bill Murray's reluctance to star in the sequel.

Harold Ramis's death in 2014 seemed to make the Ghostbusters III an even unlikelier prospect. Ivan Reitman later ruled himself out as a director of the film, but stated that he would remain onboard as the producer.

Paul Feig’s involvement gave new momentum to the project and, with Sony's blessing to reboot the franchise, the film is scheduled to start shooting in mid 2015 in New York City. At the moment, very little is known about the storyline, but Feig indicated that there will be no connection to the first two Ghostbusters films, as he doesn’t want to ruin the memory of the original franchise. He however didn’t rule out any original cast cameo roles.

Ghostbusters (if that's how it's going to be called) has a planned release date of 22 July 2016.
