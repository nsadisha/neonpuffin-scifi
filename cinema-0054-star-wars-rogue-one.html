Star Wars: Rogue One will be the first Star Wars spin-off film

The title of the first Star Wars spin-off film will be ‘Rogue One’ it has been announced by Disney chairman and CEO Bob Iger at a shareholders meeting at the Palace of Fine Arts in San Francisco on 12 March 2015.

Rogue One is the first film in a planned series of stand-alone features that will explore the characters and events of the Star Wars universe beyond the core Star Wars saga. The film will be directed by Gareth Edwards (Monsters, Godzilla) with script by Oscar nominee Chris Weitz.

The first actress cast for the Star Wars spin-off film is Felicity Jones, although no details have been released as yet on the character to be played by ‘The Theory of Everything’ star.

'Star Wars: Rogue One' will be helmed by the director of 'Godzilla' Gareth Edwards (top) and will star Academy Award nominee Felicity Jones (bottom) in yet unconfirmed role.

Little is known about the storyline of the movie, other than that the idea for the Rogue One came from John Knoll, an Academy Award-winning visual effects supervisor and chief creative officer at Industrial Light & Magic.

At the same meeting, film director Rian Johnson (Looper) has been confirmed to direct Star Wars Episode VIII.

Stand-alone Star Wars films were in the works ever since The Walt Disney Company acquired Lucasfilm back in 2012. Originally, three spin-off films were announced, with plans to release them in years between Star Wars: Episodes VII, VIII, and IX, though later only two stand-alone films were confirmed, with Lawrence Kasdan and Simon Kinberg earmarked to co-write the screenplays.

Close to nothing is known about these stand-alone Star Wars films, but rumours are abound that the first and the second spin-off film will feature Han Solo and Boba Fett respectively, especially after Jay Rasulo, Disney CFO, has described the stand-alone films as 'origin stories'.

Kathleen Kennedy, the president of Lucasfilm and the brand manager of the Star Wars franchise since The Walt Disney Company acquired the studio, said earlier that the stand-alone films will not crossover with the ‘core’ films of the series (Star Wars: Episode I – IX), stating, "George [Lucas] was so clear as to how that works. The canon that he created was the Star Wars saga. Right now, Episode VII falls within that canon. The spin-off movies, or we may come up with some other way to call those films, they exist within that vast universe that he created. There is no attempt being made to carry characters in and out of the saga episodes. Consequently, from the creative standpoint, it's a roadmap that George made pretty clear."


Star Wars: Rogue One starts shooting in summer of 2015 in London and is scheduled for theatrical release on 16 December 2016.
